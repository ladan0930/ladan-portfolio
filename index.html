Robotic Arm Gripper – Mechanical End-Effector Design

Precision gripping mechanism · Fusion 360

Project Background
  • Designed a two-finger robotic gripper capable of precision object manipulation.
  • Modeled finger geometry, linkages, pivots, and actuation paths in Fusion 360.
  • Explored lightweight structures using Fusion 360’s generative design tools.
My Role
  • Created a complete CAD assembly with joints, fasteners, and slider mechanisms.
  • Simulated gripping forces and deformation using simplified load cases.
  • Optimized finger profile to increase grip friction and reduce internal stress concentrations.
  • Produced manufacturing drawings and tolerance stack-up analysis for key components.
Results
  • Achieved a 40% mass reduction while maintaining required gripping stiffness.
  • Delivered three manufacturable design variants tailored for different payload sizes.
  • Validated reliable motion and grasp repeatability across the full stroke range.
Fusion 360 Generative Design Mechanism Design End-Effector Engineering

Snap-Fit Injection-Molded Component Redesign

Mechanical redesign & manufacturability

Scope

Results
  • Reduced part cost by 80%.
