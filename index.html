Botanical Bot – Indoor Plant Watering System

Automated irrigation robot

Project background
  • Indoor plant-watering system that maintains plant hydration without human input.
Scope
  • Develop a functional robot capable of detecting moisture levels and delivering controlled water flow.
  • Build a system using 3D-printed components and simple programmable actuation.
My role

  • Integrated motors and actuators to provide motion of the bearings.
  • Tested prototypes and iterations.
Results

  • Achieved plant detection of height and color at an 85% efficiency rate.

Drinking Bird – Ideal Heat Engine Demonstrator

Thermodynamic & mechanical analysis

Project background
  • Designed and analyzed a glass “drinking bird” heat engine to demonstrate heat transfer, phase change, and vapor-pressure-driven motion.
  • Heat-powered toy that repeatedly dips its beak into water because temperature differences move the internal evaporative liquid and keep it oscillating.
Scope
  • Break down the device into key mechanical systems (base, pivot, connecting rod, bulbs, felt head) and evaluate how each influences motion stability and cycle timing.
  • Model working-fluid behaviour to understand how pressure differentials translate into mechanical work and how geometry affects oscillation frequency.
Results
  • Designed a complete assembly with appropriate materials (glass, ABS, aluminum) to ensure stability, rigidity, and repeatable motion.
  • Verified that mass distribution, pivot placement, and bulb geometry produce a consistent tipping cycle aligned with expected pressure-driven oscillation.
  • Demonstrated how passive thermal inputs can drive continuous mechanical motion, linking thermodynamic theory to mechanical design.
Heat transfer Phase change Mechanism design

Snap-Fit Injection-Molded Component Redesign

Internship project – shim & bracket system

Background
  • Existing assemblies required durable shims to ensure alignment and load transfer in a bracket mounting system.
  • Original aluminum part was costly to machine and relied on adhesive tapes and fasteners.
Problem definition
  • New design needed to meet load requirements, maintain long-term durability, and be manufacturable without complex tooling.
Scope
  • Redesign the aluminum shim as a snap-fit injection-molded nylon component compatible with existing hardware.
  • Validate the design through calculations, prototypes, manufacturability review, and testing.
Design, prototyping & testing
  • Implemented design calculations to verify undercut size, insertion force, and allowable strain while keeping nylon within elastic limits during assembly.
  • Applied DFM: added draft angles, uniform wall thickness, and undercut features to simplify tooling and molding.
  • Created mold inserts to vary thickness without new tooling, enabling multiple products from the same mold.
  • Produced multiple 3D-printed prototypes to test engagement, undercut performance, repeatability, and fit in the existing assembly.
Results
  • Reduced part cost by 80% and cut assembly time from 20 minutes to under 5 minutes.
  • Redesigned machined aluminum components into injection-molded plastic alternatives, lowering material costs by over $12,000, reducing weight by 35%, and simplifying supplier logistics.
Injection molding Snap-fit design DFM Cost & weight reduction
